STEP 1 - LEARN ABOUT THE CUBE

If you've come to this site, chances are excellent that you are the less-than-proud owner of a Rubik's Cube that might look a little something like the mixed up Rubiks Cube to the right. You've probably just about abandoned all hope in figuring it out on your own. Random moves aren't gonna get it done. Well, you are in good company my friend - millions of people have twiddled with a Rubik's Cube for hours or days before giving up. A little factoid: If every person on earth - all 8 billion people - randomly made a single turn of their own Rubik's Cube once every second, 24 hours a day, 365 days a year, it would take on average about 170 years for just one person to stumble upon the solution!
Have no fear...Rubik's Cube Tutor is here. In just a few minutes you will be well on your way to not just solving your cube but to actually learning how to solve it on your own. But first, let's make sure that we have some basic terms defined. Along the way, you'll learn a little about the Rubik's Cube too. Here's another little factoid to get you started - there are 43,252,003,274,489,856,000 possible combinations! Don't believe me? Watch these math geeks from Numberphile explain it all in this video.

Let's begin by defining the important parts of a Rubik's cube - hover over each term to make sure you understand each one.
  • TILES - Wow - there are 54 tiles on a standard 3x3 Rubik's Cube! That 9 tiles for each of the six faces.
  • FACES - There are 6 faces (AKA "sides") on the cube. Each face has a color defined by the center tile.
  • SIDES - Just like there are 6 faces, there are six sides. However, instead of being defined by a color which never changes, they are defined by the current orientation of the cube - something that of course is subject to change without notice! Each side will be referred to in the instructions by a single letter - Upper, Front , Right, Back, Left, and Down. Remember, as you re-orient the cube, the sides will change, but the faces never will.
  • CUBE - On this website, the term "cube" will be taken to mean the moving parts that make up your whole Rubik's Cube - these of course are the center, edge, and corner cubes (defined below). There are 26 cubes that make up a Rubik's cube.
  • CENTER CUBE - Each face has a center tile with eight surrounding tiles. Each center tile should have a unique color (typically white, red, yellow, green, blue, and orange). In the instructions for solving the cube, you may see an instruction such as "bring the yellow face to the upper side."
  • EDGE CUBE - There are 12 edge pieces. Each edge cube has just two tiles.
  • CORNER CUBE - There are 8 corner pieces. Each corner cube is comprised of three tiles.

STEP 2 - COLOR THE CUBE

The Rubik's Cube tutor is here to help you not just solve your cube in a matter of minutes, The first step in the process is to color the two cubes in the left pane to match your cube. You'll notice that the first cube is a view where you can see to upper, front and left sides of the cube while the lower view is what you would see if those three sides were transparent - the back, down, and right faces.

If your cube has a yellow face, it's best to choose it as your upper face. If you don't have a yellow side, that's OK too, just choose any color you would like. Now, click or double-click on any of the six color patches below to select or adjust that color ), then click on the two cubes at left until they match your cube. If you make a mistake, just click that tile again to unset it.
